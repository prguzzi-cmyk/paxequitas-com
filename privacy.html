Privacy Policy

Effective Date: March 13, 2026  |  Last Updated: March 13, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how PaxEquitas Inc. ("PaxEquitas," "we," "us," or "our"), a Pennsylvania corporation, collects, uses, discloses, and protects your personal information when you access or use our software-as-a-service (SaaS) platform (the "Service"). This policy applies to all users of the Service, including visitors, registered users, and clients ("you" or "your").

By accessing or using the Service, you acknowledge that you have read, understood, and agree to the practices described in this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree with this Privacy Policy, please do not access or use the Service.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Account Information

When you create an account, we collect:

  • Name, email address, and password (or passkey credentials)
  • Company or business name
  • Phone number (if provided)
  • Profile information and avatar preferences
  • Authentication data from social login providers (Google, Facebook, Apple) if used

1.2 Business Information (Onboarding)

During our onboarding process, we collect business-specific information to build your knowledge graph and generate a tailored service plan. This includes:

  • Your website domain and business description
  • Industry, target audience, and geographic focus
  • Business goals, competitive landscape, and budget
  • Brand voice, messaging preferences, and content guidelines
  • Existing presence and performance data

1.3 Connected Third-Party Account Data

When you connect third-party accounts through OAuth authorization, we receive access tokens and data from those platforms. You explicitly authorize each connection and can revoke access at any time. Connected services may include:

  • Google Services: Search Console (search performance data), Google Analytics (website traffic and engagement data), Google Ads (campaign and keyword data), Google Business Profile (local business listing data), Gmail (email sending on your behalf), and YouTube (video publishing)
  • Social Media Platforms: Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, TikTok, and X (Twitter) for content publishing, engagement tracking, and advertising campaign management
  • CRM Systems: HubSpot, Salesforce, Zoho, Pipedrive, and others for customer relationship data synchronization
  • Email Providers: Gmail and Microsoft Outlook for sending emails on your behalf from your own accounts
  • Content Management Systems: WordPress, Shopify, Webflow, and others for content deployment

1.4 Website Crawl Data

When you provide your website domain, our system crawls and analyzes your publicly available website content, including page structure, meta tags, schema markup, images, performance metrics, and technical SEO elements. This data is used exclusively to provide our auditing, optimization, and service features.

1.5 Usage and Interaction Data

Pages viewed, features used, and actions taken within the Service

Chat conversations with our AI assistant

Plan approvals, rejections, and modifications

Content created, edited, or published through the Service

Device information, browser type, IP address, and access timestamps

1.6 Payment Information

Payment processing is handled by Stripe, Inc. We do not store your credit card numbers or bank account details on our servers. Stripe collects and processes your payment information in accordance with their own privacy policy. We receive and store transaction records, subscription status, and billing history.

1.7 Communication Data

If you contact us for support, provide feedback, or communicate with us through any channel, we collect the content of those communications along with your contact details.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect to:

2.1 Provide and Operate the Service

Create and manage your account

Build your business knowledge graph from onboarding data

Generate and execute AI-driven service plans

Perform website audits and technical SEO analysis

Create, schedule, and publish content across connected platforms

Manage advertising campaigns on your behalf

Send emails and communications from your connected email accounts

Generate performance reports and analytics dashboards

2.2 AI Processing

Our Service uses artificial intelligence to analyze your data, generate strategies, create content, and execute actions. Your business information, website data, and connected account data are processed by AI systems to deliver personalized services. We use one or more third-party AI service providers under enterprise agreements, and your data sent to such providers for processing is subject to their respective data usage policies.

We do not provide your raw inputs, content, or business data to any third-party AI provider for use in training their foundation models, and we configure our third-party AI integrations to disable such training use where the provider offers that option.

2.3 Operate, Secure, and Ensure the Quality of the Service

Like any software product, we cannot deliver the Service without visibility into how it is functioning. We collect and process operational data — including application logs, telemetry, error reports, feature usage and navigation analytics, AI agent execution metadata (which agents and tools ran, success and failure signals, quality scores, runtime), performance and latency metrics, security and abuse monitoring, and outcomes of actions our agents take on your behalf — for the following purposes:

  • Operate, monitor, and ensure the reliability of the Service
  • Detect, diagnose, and fix bugs, errors, and quality issues in our agents, calculations, and outputs
  • Verify the correctness of AI-generated content and recommendations
  • Investigate security incidents, abuse, and fraud
  • Identify where users encounter friction so we can improve the experience
  • This operational data may include personal information to the extent it is inherent in the operation of the Service. We treat it with the same confidentiality and security protections that apply to your other account data.

2.4 Improve the Service Using De-identified Data

We may generate de-identified data from your account data, content, AI inputs and outputs, and operational data, and use that de-identified data on an ongoing basis to:

  • Evaluate, tune, and improve our proprietary AI agents, prompts, workflows, and orchestration logic
  • Build and refine the evaluation datasets and quality models we use to test the Service
  • Develop, benchmark, and test new features
  • Produce aggregated industry insights and statistics
  • "De-identified" means we strip direct and indirect identifiers and aggregate or otherwise modify the data so that it cannot reasonably be used to identify you, your business, your team, or your customers. We do not attempt to re-identify de-identified data, and we do not combine it with other data in a way that would permit re-identification.

2.5 Communications

Send service-related notifications (agent activity updates, plan approvals needed, task completions)

Provide customer support

Send promotional communications (with your consent; you may opt out at any time)

2.6 Legal and Safety

Comply with applicable laws and regulations

Enforce our Terms of Service

Protect the rights, safety, and security of our users and the Service

Detect and prevent fraud, abuse, and security incidents

We share data with third-party service providers who perform services on our behalf, subject to confidentiality obligations:

We may disclose your information if required to do so by law or in response to valid legal process, including subpoenas, court orders, or government requests. If PaxEquitas is involved in a merger, acquisition, reorganization, or sale of assets, your information may be transferred as part of that transaction. We will notify you via email and/or a prominent notice on the Service of any change in ownership or uses of your personal information.

4. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organizational security measures to protect your personal information against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. These measures include:

  • Encryption of data in transit (TLS/SSL) and at rest
  • Secure OAuth 2.0 token storage with encryption for all third-party connections
  • Role-based access controls and authentication (including passkey/WebAuthn support)
  • Regular security assessments and monitoring
  • Secure cloud infrastructure hosted on AWS
  • While we strive to protect your information, no method of transmission over the Internet or method of electronic storage is 100% secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security.

5. Data Retention

We retain your personal information for as long as your account is active or as needed to provide the Service. If you close your account, we will delete or anonymize your personal information within 90 days, except where we are required to retain it for legal, accounting, or regulatory purposes.

Third-party OAuth tokens are stored only while your account is active and the connection is authorized. When you disconnect a third-party account or close your PaxEquitas account, associated tokens are deleted.

Website crawl data, plans, and content created through the Service are retained for the duration of your subscription. Aggregated, anonymized data that cannot be used to identify you may be retained indefinitely for analytics and service improvement purposes.

14. Facebook and Instagram (Meta) Data

14.1 Data We Collect from Meta Platforms

When you connect your Facebook Page or Instagram Business account to PaxEquitas, we collect and process the following data through the Meta Graph API:

  • Page Information: Page name, category, profile picture, follower count, and page access tokens
  • Instagram Business Account Information: Username, profile picture, follower count, and account ID
  • Post Data: Posts, comments, reactions, shares, and engagement metrics for content published through PaxEquitas
  • Page Insights: Reach, impressions, engagement rates, and audience demographics for your connected Pages and Instagram accounts
  • Comments and Messages: Comments on your posts and direct messages (only when you authorize comment management features)

14.2 How We Use Meta Data

We use data from your connected Facebook and Instagram accounts exclusively to:

  • Publish content (posts, images, videos, carousels) to your Pages and Instagram Business account on your behalf
  • Monitor and report on post performance and engagement metrics
  • Manage comments and engagement on your published content
  • Generate AI-powered insights and recommendations based on your audience data
  • Optimize content strategy based on performance analytics
  • We do not sell, rent, or share your Facebook or Instagram data with third parties for their own purposes. We do not use your Meta data to build advertising profiles or for purposes unrelated to providing the PaxEquitas service.

14.3 Data Retention and Deletion

Facebook and Instagram data is retained only while your account is active and your Meta account connection is authorized. When you disconnect your Facebook or Instagram account from PaxEquitas:

  • All associated access tokens are immediately deleted
  • Cached page and account metadata is deleted within 24 hours
  • Published content remains on the respective Meta platform under your control — PaxEquitas does not retain copies of published content
  • Performance analytics data is retained in aggregate form for reporting purposes but is anonymized within 90 days of disconnection

14.4 Meta Data Deletion Requests

In compliance with Meta Platform Terms, we provide a data deletion callback. When you remove PaxEquitas from your Facebook account settings, Meta notifies us automatically and we delete all data associated with your Meta account.

You can also request deletion of your Meta-related data at any time by:

  • Disconnecting your Facebook/Instagram account in your PaxEquitas dashboard settings
  • Contacting us at the email address below with the subject "Meta Data Deletion Request"
  • Removing PaxEquitas from your Facebook account at Settings & Privacy → Settings → Apps and Websites
  • All deletion requests are processed within 48 hours.

14.5 Meta Platform Compliance

Our use of Facebook and Instagram data is governed by Meta's Platform Terms and Developer Policies. We comply with all applicable Meta policies regarding data access, usage, storage, and deletion. We do not:

  • Transfer Meta data to data brokers, information brokers, or any other service involved in data monetization
  • Use Meta data for surveillance purposes
  • Store Meta access tokens in unencrypted form — all tokens are encrypted at rest using AES-256 encryption
  • Use Meta data to discriminate against any individual or group
